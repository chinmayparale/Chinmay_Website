Ventricular Tachycardia 3D Mapping Ablation

3D Mapping Guided Ablation for Incessant Scar-Related Ventricular Tachycardia

Patient Presentation

A middle-aged male with a significant previous history of coronary artery disease presented with incessant ventricular tachycardia (VT) and decompensated congestive cardiac failure, refractory to standard medical therapies.

Advanced Technique & Intervention

High-density electroanatomic mapping was performed using the state-of-the-art CARTO 3D system to delineate the arrhythmogenic scar substrate. Targeted radiofrequency ablation was delivered to critical conduction isthmuses sustaining the VT circuits.

Clinical Outcome

Complete termination of clinical ventricular tachycardia with successful non-inducibility post-procedure, facilitating stable hemodynamic recovery and discharge.

Atrial Fibrillation Pulmonary Vein Isolation

Pulmonary Vein Isolation (PVI) for Paroxysmal Atrial Fibrillation

Patient Presentation

A middle-aged patient experiencing highly symptomatic, recurrent paroxysmal atrial fibrillation associated with secondary heart failure symptoms and reduced functional capacity.

Advanced Technique & Intervention

The patient underwent specialized catheter ablation utilizing comprehensive 3D electroanatomic mapping to achieve precise, circumferential Pulmonary Vein Isolation (PVI), targeting the primary trigger sites for atrial fibrillation.

Clinical Outcome

Successful electrical isolation of all four pulmonary veins, achieving immediate restoration and long-term maintenance of normal sinus rhythm alongside complete resolution of heart failure symptoms.
